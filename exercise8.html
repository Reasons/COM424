Exercise 8

Color

Description:

For Exercise 8, revisit the work you completed for Exercise 5: Symmetry and Asymmetry. Select the composition you created for Exercise 5 that you think was most successful and revise using the design strategy of color to add the dimension of depth to your design. If you already introduced color in your work for Exercise 5, try adjusting the color scheme to produce a different visual impression from the design. Rather than using the entire design you created for Exercise 5, crop the design to an visually interesting detail where adjusting color creates the most visual impact for the viewer.

Depth perception can be greatly accepted by color, as changes in color are easy to spot and occur naturally, namely in the presence of light or darkness. In the presence or lack of light, the brightness of the perceived color changes based on how much light there is. In the digital world, this is simple to recreate, changing the brightness/contrast of a color to gradually make it seem lighter or darker. In some cases it may even appear as if it is fading away. In color theory, complimentary colors are on the opposite ends of the color wheel and when you see it, it is a direct clash, so the colors "pop" more.
